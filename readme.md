# Dear Friend

![cover](./presskit/cover.png)

**Dear Friend** is a 3D interactive storytelling project developed in Unity.

The project explores care, memory, grief, friendship, and intergenerational connection through the relationship between a student and an elderly resident living in the same intergenerational building.

The story follows Camille, a student who helps Anna, an elderly resident, free space on her computer. What begins as a simple practical task slowly becomes more intimate. By opening, sorting, and deleting files, Camille discovers fragments of Anna’s life and learns about a close friend Anna recently lost.

The experience is not designed as a traditional game. It is closer to an interactive story, where the player is asked to observe, listen, hesitate, and reflect on what it means to care for someone through small gestures.

## Project Intentions

This project focuses on care as something quiet and everyday. Care is not shown through heroic actions, but through presence, attention, listening, and respect for someone else’s memories.

The main interaction, deleting files from someone else’s computer, creates a simple but meaningful dilemma:
should the player complete the task efficiently, or slow down and take responsibility for what they discover?

Through this interaction, the project asks:

* What does it mean to help someone let go?
* When does helping become intrusive?
* What traces of a life should be kept, hidden, or erased?
* How can a small digital gesture become an act of care?

## Context

**Dear Friend** was created as part of the **Care Workshop** in the **Master Media Design** program at HEAD Genève.

The project was informed by research on intergenerational living, including interviews with residents of Maison de l’Adret, an intergenerational building in Geneva.

## Content Note

The story deals with grief, memory, aging, and assisted suicide through the organization Exit. These themes are approached through Anna’s personal experience and are treated with care and restraint.
